Creative Bio

Elena Rostova

Food Stylist, Photographer & Creative Culinary Director

Crafting luxury, organic visual stories for Michelin-starred chefs, global culinary books, and premium FMCG packaging campaigns from my custom-built daylight studio kitchen in New York.

The Story

Designing with Real Flavors

For over a decade, I have bridged the gap between culinary art and visual media. My work is defined by an uncompromising commitment to realism, texture, and natural lighting. Rather than relying on synthetic glues or heavy chemicals that destroy the food integrity, I utilize advanced heat controls, raw ingredient mapping, and high-speed photography strobes to capture dishes exactly as they should be enjoyed: fresh, organic, and delicious.

Background

From Culinary Arts to the Lens

My journey began in the kitchens of classic French bistros, where I trained as a pastry apprentice. I fell in love with the temporary architecture of plating—the delicate balance of colors, heights, and textures on porcelain.

Realizing that these edible masterpieces lasted only moments before being served, I picked up a medium-format camera to document them. That shift defined my career: translating the multi-sensory experience of taste into an immersive visual frame.

Core Principles

Our Styling Philosophy

01 / Honest Textures

We celebrate the natural state of food. A crumb of bread, the rustic crack of sugar glaze, or glistening olive oil. We do not spray synthetic lacquer; we preserve real freshness.

02 / Organic Lighting

Natural window light is our primary canvas. We reinforce it with soft studio diffusers to keep shadow transitions organic, reflecting real atmosphere.

03 / Spatial Harmony

Every element in the frame must serve the main culinary component. We apply negative space, hand-woven linens, and minimal ceramics to direct client focus.

Help Center

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you travel for restaurant or editorial shoots?

+
Yes, we shoot worldwide. While our main daylight kitchen studio is located in New York, we routinely pack specialized mobile lighting and prop kits for assignments in Europe, Asia, and other regions in North America. Travel rates apply.

Do you handle prop sourcing and ingredients?

+
Absolutely. As part of our food styling services, we manage prop procurement (ceramics, utensils, fabrics) and hand-select fresh produce, high-grade organic cuts, and herbs from local premium suppliers before shooting day.

What medium format camera systems do you use?

+
For high-end commercial packaging and large format print billboards, we capture using Phase One XF IQ4 150MP or Fujifilm GFX 100S medium format cameras to ensure maximum dynamic range and color accuracy.

What is your turnaround time for retouched image delivery?

+
Standard turnaround is 10 business days from the final image selection. Rush delivery options (48-72 hours) are available for time-sensitive commercial launches and can be quoted upon request.
